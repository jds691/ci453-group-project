    What is cyber security?

    Cyber security is the means by which individuals and organisations reduce the risk of being affected by cyber crime.

    Cyber security's core function is to protect the devices we all use (smartphones, laptops, tablets and computers), and the services we access online - both at home and work - from theft or damage. It's also about preventing unauthorised access to the vast amounts of personal information we store on these devices, and online.

    Cyber security is important because smartphones, computers and the internet are now such a fundamental part of modern life, that it's difficult to imagine how we'd function without them. From online banking and shopping, to email and social media, it's more important than ever to take steps that can prevent cyber criminals getting hold of our accounts, data, and devices.

    This website will go over key concepts in Computer, Information and network security as well as the impact of AI on cyber security.

    Steps to keep yourself safe online:

    1. Use strong and separate passwords - strong passwords make it harder for hackers to guess or brute-force your password and separate passwords stop them from gaining access to other accounts if they only have one password.
    2. Install the latest software and app updates - these updates often contain patches that fix any weaknesses in the code that hackers could exploit.
    3. Turn on 2-Step verification (2SV) - an extra step to log in means hackers cannot gain access with only a password.
    4. Password managers - store passwords so you don't lose or forget them, allow you to use stronger passwords that may be harder to remember and make it easier to log in without having to try lots of passwords.
    5. Back up your data - no one wants to lose photos or contacts, back them up to the cloud so they are accessible if your device gets stolen/lost.

    Passwords

    1. 3 random words
    2. At least 8 characters
    3. Containing uppercase, lowercase, numbers and special characters
    4. Do not use name, date of birth, pet name or any easily guessable information.
